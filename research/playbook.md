# LinkedIn Content Playbook (B2B SaaS)

## Core Recommendations (with Sources)

### 1. Start with a strong hook

One thing I noticed pretty quickly is how much the first line matters.

Example:
“If you find something that works, your only job is to do more of it.”

Why it works:
- It makes you stop scrolling almost instantly  
- You already expect a clear takeaway

### 2. Write in short, scannable lines

Almost none of the posts I looked at used long paragraphs.

Most of them are:
- short lines  
- spaced out  
- really easy to skim  

Why it works:
- feels lighter to read  
- works better on mobile  

Source: Lara Acosta

### 3. Combine personal story with a clear takeaway

A pattern I kept seeing is:
start with something personal → then turn it into a lesson.

Why it works:
- feels more real  
- easier to relate to  
- doesn’t feel like you’re being lectured

- gets attention fast  
- invites reactions  

But from what I see:
- it only really works if you already have some credibility

## Where Experts Disagree

### 1. Aggressive vs relatable hooks

- Alex Hormozi → very direct  
- Lara Acosta → more personal and relatable  

My take:  
Relatable hooks feel more sustainable long term.  
The aggressive style works, but not everyone can pull it off consistently.

---

### 2. Opinion vs education

- Dave Gerhardt → strong opinions  
- Amanda Natividad → more structured and educational  

My take:  
If you're just starting, educational content feels safer.  
Opinion works better once you already have some authority.

---

### 3. Simple vs strategic content

- Matt Gray → simple frameworks  
- Ross Simmonds → more strategic  

My take:  
Simple works better early on.  
Strategy becomes more important once you’re scaling.

---

## What I Rejected and Why

### 1. Long, dense posts

Some posts go really deep and long.

Why I didn’t include it:
- harder to read on mobile  
- people are more likely to drop off  

---

### 2. Story without a clear takeaway

Some posts tell a story but don’t clearly explain the point.

Why I didn’t include it:
- feels incomplete  
- you finish reading without getting much value  

---

## My Original Idea

One thing I didn’t see explicitly mentioned:

**Content Loop System**

Instead of always coming up with new ideas:
- take a few core ideas  
- reuse them in different formats:
  - story  
  - list  
  - opinion  

Why I think this works:
- makes content more sustainable  
- reinforces positioning  
- matches what top creators are already doing  

---

## Weaknesses of This Playbook

- based on observation, not tested execution  
- assumes LinkedIn behavior stays the same  
- doesn’t go deep into distribution systems  
- might not apply to very niche audiences
